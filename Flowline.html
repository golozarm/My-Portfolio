Full Wafer · Top Layer
01 — Wafer-Scale Device

Electro-Optical Array Chip

A full wafer layout integrating three routed layers on a single substrate: an outer ring of bond pads for electrical interconnect, a branching fluidic tree feeding a dense reaction-well array, and fine-pitch routing threading between both without collision. Every trace had to resolve a competing claim on the same real estate — power, fluid, and signal sharing one wafer.

100mm wafer Multi-layer routing Photomask-ready
Etch Mask · Channel Detail
02 — Etch Mask

Etched Serpentine Channel

A single continuous channel folded back on itself hundreds of times to pack maximum residence length into a fixed wafer footprint — the geometry problem at the center of most lab-on-chip thermal and mixing designs. The density here is the point: more fold, more path, more time for the fluid to do its work before it reaches the outlet.

Wet/DRIE etch mask High aspect density Single continuous path
Machinist Drawing · Sheet 1
03 — Precision Manifold

Fluidic Distribution Manifold

A dimensioned machinist drawing for a milled aluminum manifold — cross-section, port threading, and channel face on one sheet. Tapped ports are toleranced for barbed fittings, the channel floor calls out end-mill diameter and depth directly, and flatness is held to 0.001" where the cover seals. This is the handoff document a shop floor actually runs from.

10-32 tapped ports ±0.001" flatness CNC-ready, DXF provided

04 — Precision Manifold, Cover

Manifold Cover & Comb Array

The mating half of the manifold above, where a serpentine feed transitions into a fine comb of parallel micro-channels — spreading flow evenly across a cavity before it reaches the working chamber. Both sheets were dimensioned to close tolerance so the two halves seal as one fluidic part, not two approximations of one.

2× 10-32 tapped ports 90° V-groove, 0.025" deep Mating sheet pair
Test Wafer · Comparative Study
05 — Design-of-Experiments Wafer

Comparative Geometry Test Set

A single wafer carrying parallel variants of the same design side by side — three reaction-chamber geometries at different aspect ratios, paired branching-channel structures at two trace widths, and matched long-path serpentine pairs. Running variants together on one wafer is how you isolate which geometry actually drives the result, instead of guessing from a single build.

Multi-variant DOE Parametric chamber geometry Single-run comparison

06 — Early Wafer Layout

Dual-Arm Channel & Alignment Grid

A symmetric pair of folded channel arms converging on a shared outlet, set inside a full radial alignment grid used to register the pattern against subsequent mask layers. The grid itself is as load-bearing as the channel: without it, nothing lines up downstream in a multi-mask process.
